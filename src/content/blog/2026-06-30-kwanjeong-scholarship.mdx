When I was applying for PhD programs, I also started looking for scholarships that could fund my research.

Unfortunately, only a limited number of scholarships are open to international students, so I also looked in my country of passport, South Korea. I found several that fund overseas PhD research:

- <Link href='https://www.iljufoundation.org/html/scholarship/fs/list/ussk.do' isExternal>Ilju Academy and Culture Foundation Scholarship</Link>
- <Link href='https://apply.kfas.or.kr/program/list.php?service_type=1' isExternal>Korea Foundation for Advanced Studies Scholarship</Link>
- <Link href='https://foundation.sbs.co.kr/scholarship/introduce/' isExternal>SBS Foundation Scholarship</Link>
- <Link href='https://www.ikef.or.kr/page.php?id=/kj/scholar_schedule' isExternal>Kwanjeong Foundation Scholarship</Link>

However, since I received my high school diploma and bachelor's degrees overseas, the Kwanjeong Foundation Scholarship was the only one I was eligible for. 

The application process was time-consuming but fairly straightforward (feel free to contact me if you would like to see my statement of purpose---I'd recommend preparing for it in advance).

After passing the first and second round of reviews, the final round was an in-person 30-minute group interview, with multiple interviewers and multiple interviewees. The questions they asked were along these lines (the interview is conducted in Korean):

- A one-minute introduction.
- (How did your hear about us? Did you apply for other scholarships?)
- What is your major, and what is your subfield? Briefly explain your reserach topic.
- (Your subfield seems new. Does it exist in Korea?)
- (How does your research topic contribute/benefit Korea?)
- How is AI used in your research?
- Why did you choose this university (or this country?)
- What are your future career plans?
- What do you like to do in your free time?
- A 30-second outro.

I only prepared for one-minute introduction, and familiarized myself with the <Link href='https://www.ikef.or.kr/page.php?id=/info/info' isExternal>foundation's history</Link>. I think being honest, consistent, and concise is important in these interviews.
